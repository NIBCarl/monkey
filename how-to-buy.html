Step-by-Step Guide

1

Create a Wallet

First, you'll need a wallet that supports Ethereum (ERC-20) tokens. We recommend MetaMask for its ease of use and widespread compatibility.

Desktop:

  1. Visit metamask.io and download the browser extension
  2. Follow the setup instructions to create a new wallet
  3. Be sure to securely store your seed phrase

Mobile:

  1. Download the MetaMask app from the App Store or Google Play
  2. Create a new wallet following the in-app instructions
  3. Ensure you keep your recovery phrase safe

2

Get ETH

You'll need Ethereum (ETH) to swap for EMERSON and to pay for transaction fees (gas).

Option 1: Buy directly in your wallet

  1. Open your MetaMask wallet
  2. Click on "Buy" and select your preferred payment method
  3. Follow the instructions to purchase ETH

Option 2: Buy from an exchange

  1. Purchase ETH on an exchange like Coinbase, Binance, or Kraken
  2. Withdraw the ETH to your MetaMask wallet address
  3. Ensure you're using the Ethereum network for the transfer
3

Connect to Uniswap

Uniswap is a decentralized exchange (DEX) that allows you to swap ETH for EMERSON.

  1. Visit app.uniswap.org in your browser
  2. Click "Connect Wallet" in the top right corner
  3. Select MetaMask (or your preferred wallet)
  4. Approve the connection request in your wallet

4

Swap ETH for EMERSON

Now it's time to make the swap and become an EMERSON holder!

  1. On Uniswap, select ETH in the "From" field
  2. For the "To" field, click "Select token" and paste the EMERSON contract address
  3. Enter the amount of ETH you want to swap
  4. Click "Swap" and confirm the transaction in your wallet
  5. Wait for the transaction to be confirmed on the blockchain

Make sure to set your slippage tolerance to 2-3% to account for price movements and taxes.

5

Add EMERSON to Your Wallet

After completing your purchase, you may need to add EMERSON as a custom token to view it in your wallet.

  1. In MetaMask, click the "Assets" tab and scroll down to click "Import tokens"
  2. Select "Custom Token" and paste the EMERSON contract address
  3. The token symbol (EMERSON) and decimals (18) should auto-fill
  4. Click "Add Custom Token" and then "Import Tokens"
  5. You should now see your EMERSON balance in your wallet!

Common Questions

What is gas and how much will I need?

Gas is a fee paid to Ethereum network miners to process your transaction. Gas fees fluctuate based on network congestion. We recommend having at least $30-50 worth of ETH for the transaction and gas fees combined. You can check current gas prices at Etherscan Gas Tracker.

Why is my transaction pending or failed?

Transactions can be delayed or fail due to network congestion, insufficient gas, or slippage issues. If your transaction is pending for a long time, you can try increasing the gas price to speed it up, or if it failed, check if you had enough ETH for the gas fees and consider increasing your slippage tolerance (especially during high volatility).

How do I know if I'm buying the real EMERSON?

Always triple-check the contract address to ensure you're buying the authentic EMERSON token. Scammers often create fake tokens with similar names. The only legitimate contract address is the one provided on this website and in our official social media channels.

Will EMERSON be available on centralized exchanges?

We're actively working on securing listings on centralized exchanges (CEXs) to make buying EMERSON even easier. These developments will be announced through our official channels when confirmed. For now, Uniswap and other DEXs are the primary way to purchase.
